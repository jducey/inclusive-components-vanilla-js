A Content Slider

Control

In the broadest terms, any inclusive component should be:

  • Clear and easy to use
  • Interoperable with different inputs and outputs
  • Responsive and device-agnostic
  • Performant
  • Under the user's control

Multimodal interaction

Multimodal means "can be operated in different ways." Browsers are naturally multimodal. Make sure your content cand be operated by mouse, keyboard and touch.

Basic slider without JavaScript

Some browsers hide the scrollbar until the content is focused on by the user. In order to create the needed affordance that an always visible scrollbar will achieve specific styles with cross-browser support are added.

To make our slider element focusable by the user, we need to add tabindex="0". Since the (foused) element will now be announced in screen readers, we ought to give it a role and label, identifying it.

A basic slider with instructions

To support multimodal interaction the instructions will appear on hover, focus and touch. Touch is dependent on a small amount of JavaScript.
